# EduVault — Privacy & Compliance

## FERPA Compliance

EduVault is fully compliant with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). Our platform is designed to protect the privacy of student education records.

### FERPA Commitments
- EduVault acts as a "school official" with a "legitimate educational interest" under FERPA guidelines
- No student education records are disclosed to third parties without prior written consent from the institution
- All employees with access to student data undergo FERPA training during onboarding and annually thereafter
- Students may request access to their own education records through their institution's designated process
- Parents of dependent students may access records through institutional procedures

### Data Classification
EduVault classifies all data handled by the platform:
- **Restricted**: Student PII, grades, disciplinary records, financial aid data
- **Confidential**: Institutional configuration, API keys, internal reports
- **Internal**: Non-sensitive operational data, usage analytics (aggregated)
- **Public**: Marketing materials, public documentation

### Directory Information
EduVault does not independently designate directory information. Each institution configures which fields are considered directory information per their FERPA policy.

## COPPA Compliance

While EduVault primarily serves higher education institutions, the platform does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If an institution serves K-12 students, a COPPA-specific data processing addendum is executed.

## State Privacy Laws

EduVault monitors and complies with applicable state student privacy laws including:
- California: SOPIPA (Student Online Personal Information Protection Act)
- New York: Education Law 2-D
- Colorado: Student Data Transparency and Security Act
- Illinois: SOPPA (Student Online Personal Protection Act)

## GDPR Readiness

For institutions serving students from the European Economic Area (EEA):
- EduVault can execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Data subject rights are supported (access, rectification, erasure, portability)
- Data processing is based on contractual necessity with the institution
- No data is transferred outside the agreed hosting region without adequate safeguards

## Data Processing Agreement

EduVault provides a standard Data Processing Agreement (DPA) to all institutional customers that includes:
- Scope and purpose of data processing
- Data retention and deletion terms
- Security obligations
- Sub-processor disclosures
- Breach notification procedures
- Audit rights for institutions

## Data Retention

### During Active Contract
- All student records retained for the duration of the contract
- Audit logs retained for 3 years
- System logs retained for 1 year

### After Contract Termination
- All institution data is deleted within 90 days of contract termination
- Institution may request a full data export in standard formats (CSV, JSON) before termination
- Upon written request, data can be deleted within 30 days
- A certificate of data destruction is provided upon completion
- Backup data is purged within 120 days of contract termination

## Privacy by Design

EduVault follows privacy-by-design principles:
- Data minimization: Only collect data necessary for platform functionality
- Purpose limitation: Data used only for stated educational purposes
- Access controls: Least-privilege access model
- Anonymization: Analytics and reporting use aggregated, de-identified data
- Transparency: Clear privacy notices for all data collection points

## Sub-Processors

EduVault uses the following sub-processors:
- **AWS**: Cloud infrastructure and data hosting (US regions)
- **SendGrid**: Transactional email delivery (no student PII in emails)
- **Datadog**: Application monitoring (no student PII in logs)

Institutions are notified 30 days before any new sub-processor is added. Institutions may object to new sub-processors per the terms of the DPA.
